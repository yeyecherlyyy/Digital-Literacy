## Overview
This task involved learning and applying **professional email etiquette** and developing **social media guidelines** for responsible online communication.

## Part 2 – Social Media Guidelines

### Professional Social Media Guidelines

#### ✅ Do's
1. **Be respectful** – Treat all users with courtesy and professionalism.
2. **Think before you post** – Consider the impact of your content before sharing.
3. **Protect privacy** – Avoid sharing personal or sensitive information publicly.
4. **Give credit** – Always attribute ideas, images, and content to the original creator.
5. **Stay accurate** – Only share verified and factual information.
6. **Use appropriate language** – Maintain a professional and inclusive tone.
7. **Secure your accounts** – Use strong passwords and enable two-factor authentication.

#### ❌ Don'ts
1. **Do not cyberbully** – Never harass, threaten, or demean others online.
2. **Do not overshare** – Avoid posting your location, daily routine, or financial details.
3. **Do not spread misinformation** – Always verify news before sharing.
4. **Do not engage with trolls** – Ignore or block negative users instead of retaliating.
5. **Do not use copyrighted material without permission** – Respect intellectual property rights.
6. **Do not post offensive content** – Content that is discriminatory, hateful, or inappropriate is unacceptable.

---

## Key Takeaways
- Professional email communication reflects your personal brand and work ethic.
- Clear, concise, and respectful emails build strong professional relationships.
- Social media is a powerful tool — using it responsibly protects your reputation and others' wellbeing.
- Following email and social media best practices is a core component of digital literacy.
