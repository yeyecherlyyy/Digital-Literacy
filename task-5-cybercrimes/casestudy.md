# Cybercrime Case Study: Phishing Attack

## What is Phishing?
Phishing is a type of cybercrime in which attackers try to trick individuals into sharing sensitive information such as passwords, bank details, or personal data. They usually pretend to be a trusted source like a company, bank, or institution.

## How It Happens (Step-by-Step)
1. A student receives an email claiming to be from a well-known company offering an internship.
2. The email appears professional, with proper formatting and logos.
3. It creates urgency by asking the student to apply quickly through a link.
4. The link redirects to a fake website that looks genuine.
5. The student enters personal or login details, which are then captured by the attacker.

## Who is Targeted?
- Students looking for internships or job opportunities  
- People who trust professional-looking emails  
- Users who act quickly without verifying the source  

## Consequences
- Personal accounts may be hacked  
- Financial loss can occur  
- Sensitive information may be misused  
- Damage to digital identity and reputation  

## Key Takeaway
Always verify the source before clicking on links or sharing personal information.  
Be cautious of urgent messages and offers that seem too good to be true.
