## Part 1 – Case Study: Phishing Attack

### Title: The 2020 Twitter Bitcoin Scam – A Social Engineering & Phishing Attack

### Background
In July 2020, a massive cyberattack targeted **Twitter**, one of the world's largest social media platforms. Hackers gained access to high-profile accounts including those of **Elon Musk**, **Barack Obama**, **Apple**, and **Kanye West**.

### How It Happened
The attackers used **social engineering** to manipulate Twitter employees into providing access to internal administrative tools. Once inside, they:
- Took control of verified accounts with millions of followers
- Posted fraudulent messages promising to double Bitcoin payments
- Directed followers to send Bitcoin to a specific wallet address

### Type of Cybercrime
- **Social Engineering** – Manipulating people to reveal confidential information
- **Account Hijacking** – Unauthorized access to user accounts
- **Financial Fraud / Scam** – Deceiving victims into sending money

### Impact
| Category | Impact |
|----------|--------|
| Financial Loss | Over $120,000 USD in Bitcoin stolen from victims |
| Reputation | Severe damage to Twitter's brand and credibility |
| Legal | Multiple arrests made; legal investigations launched |
| User Trust | Millions of users lost trust in platform security |

### Lessons Learned
1. Even large corporations are vulnerable to insider threats and social engineering.
2. Employee cybersecurity training is critical.
3. Multi-layered security systems are necessary to prevent unauthorized access.
4. Users must verify information before taking financial action online.

## Part 2 – Cybercrime Prevention Checklist

Use this checklist to protect yourself and your organization from cyber threats:

### 🔐 Account Security
- [ ] Use strong, unique passwords for every account (minimum 12 characters)
- [ ] Enable **Two-Factor Authentication (2FA)** on all accounts
- [ ] Use a trusted **password manager** to store credentials safely
- [ ] Never share passwords with anyone
- [ ] Regularly update passwords, especially after a data breach

### 📧 Email & Phishing Protection
- [ ] Verify the sender's email address before clicking any links
- [ ] Do not open attachments from unknown senders
- [ ] Hover over links to preview the URL before clicking
- [ ] Report suspicious emails to your IT/security team
- [ ] Do not provide personal information in response to unsolicited emails

### 🌐 Safe Browsing
- [ ] Only visit websites with **HTTPS** in the URL
- [ ] Avoid using public Wi-Fi for sensitive transactions
- [ ] Use a **VPN** when connecting to public networks
- [ ] Clear browser cookies and cache regularly
- [ ] Keep browsers updated to the latest version

### 💻 Device & Software Security
- [ ] Keep your operating system and software up-to-date
- [ ] Install reputable **antivirus/anti-malware** software
- [ ] Avoid downloading software from unofficial sources
- [ ] Lock your device when not in use
- [ ] Regularly back up important data to a secure location

### 📱 Social Media Safety
- [ ] Review and tighten privacy settings on all platforms
- [ ] Be cautious about what personal information you share publicly
- [ ] Avoid accepting connection requests from unknown individuals
- [ ] Log out of accounts on shared or public devices
- [ ] Monitor your accounts for suspicious activity

### 🏢 Organizational Security
- [ ] Conduct regular cybersecurity awareness training for employees
- [ ] Implement role-based access control (least privilege principle)
- [ ] Have an **incident response plan** in place
- [ ] Conduct regular security audits and penetration testing
- [ ] Report any security incidents immediately to the appropriate authority

---

## Key Takeaways
- Cybercrime is a growing global threat affecting individuals, organizations, and governments.
- Most cyberattacks exploit human error — education and awareness are the best defenses.
- Preventive measures like strong passwords, 2FA, and safe browsing habits significantly reduce risk.
- Everyone has a responsibility to practice good cybersecurity hygiene.
